Other Quotients

(SQ, EQ, AQ)

Beyond the traditional Intelligence Quotient (IQ), understanding Emotional Quotient (EQ), Social Quotient (SQ), and Adversity Quotient (AQ) provides a comprehensive view of an individual's capabilities in various aspects of life.

Emotional Quotient (EQ)

EQ refers to the ability to recognize, understand, and manage one's own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. It comprises several key components:

  • Self-Awareness: Recognizing and understanding one's own emotions.
  • Self-Regulation: Managing or redirecting disruptive emotions and impulses.
  • Motivation: Being driven to achieve for the sake of achievement.
  • Empathy: Recognizing, understanding, and considering other people's feelings, especially when making decisions.
  • Social Skills: Managing relationships to guide, influence, and develop others.

Social Quotient (SQ)

SQ measures an individual's ability to interact and communicate effectively with others. Key aspects include:

  • Social Awareness: Understanding social dynamics and norms.
  • Interpersonal Skills: Building and maintaining healthy relationships.
  • Communication Skills: Effectively conveying and receiving messages.
  • Conflict Resolution: Navigating and resolving disagreements constructively.

Adversity Quotient (AQ)

AQ assesses how well an individual withstands adversities and challenges. Its components are:

  • Control: The extent to which one perceives having control over adverse events.
  • Ownership: Taking responsibility for outcomes, regardless of external circumstances.
  • Reach: Understanding how far the impact of adversity extends into other areas of life.
  • Endurance: Persistence and resilience.
